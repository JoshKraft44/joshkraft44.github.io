About Me

I care deeply about self-improvement and entrepreneurship, and I enjoy tackling meaningful, difficult, and nuanced problems.

Background

I'm currently studying Computer Science at the University of Calgary, with a strong focus on entrepreneurship. My journey into computer science stemmed from an obsessive enjoyment of mathematics, problem-solving, and building things. Over time, I've become especially interested in how technology and business intersect — whether that's through coding full-scale applications, launching startups, or exploring other creative projects.

I co-founded a small business during the pandemic, which taught me more about logistics, marketing, and adaptability than any textbook could. Alongside that, I've worked on personal and collaborative coding projects, always seeking ways to improve, learn, and create something meaningful. I'm particularly drawn to clean design, user-centered thinking, and building intuitive interfaces — as well as solving hard backend problems that make everything tick.

I also work part time as a clinic assistant at a variety of medical clinics in Calgary and the surrounding area. This has broadened my perspective and improved my communication skills, while teaching me a lot about the intersection between technology and health care.
